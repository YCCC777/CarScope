💡 Key tip: Pay $100–150 for a Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) by an independent mechanic before buying. It can save you thousands.
Before you visit
📄 Run a CARFAX or AutoCheck report (~$40) — accident history, title issues, odometer
🔧 Book a pre-purchase inspection with an independent mechanic ($100–150)
🛡️ Get insurance quotes before you commit — rates vary a lot by car model
🔍 Search "[year] [make] [model] common problems" online
At the dealership
🚗 Check panel gaps on hood, doors, trunk — uneven gaps signal past collision repairs
🦀 Look for rust under the car, in wheel wells, and along door edges
🔢 Confirm VIN on dashboard matches the title and door jamb sticker
🛞 Check tire tread depth and wear pattern — uneven wear = alignment or suspension issue
💡 Test every feature: A/C, heat, all windows, locks, mirrors, Bluetooth, USB ports
🔦 Check all lights: headlights, brake lights, turn signals, reverse lights
🏎️ Test drive at least 15 min — include highway speed, hard braking, sharp turns
👂 Cold-start the engine and listen for unusual noises before the test drive
Documents to request
📋 Clean title (not "salvage", "rebuilt", or "flood")
📋 Maintenance and service records
📋 As-is receipt or warranty documentation
